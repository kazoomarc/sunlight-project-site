    ## Integration: Change of variable
    We want to calculate the sum <br/>
        $$y = \int{f(x)}dx$$

        Here we have to integrate $f(x)$. But as expected at least we may not be able to integrate $\f(x)$
        <br/>
        The solution here is to form an integral which exists to integrate!
        <br/>
        The idea is that we take some of the variables of $x$ pack them up into a single variate. This will hopefully
        produce a new function which is easier to integrate plus some easily calculate arithmetic.

        <br/>
        Essentially we look at our integral as

        $$y = \int{f(x)}dx = \int{f(x)\frac{dx}{du}}du$$

        We choose $u$ such that can replace all the $x$ in $f(x) -> g(u)$. The idea is to choose $u$ so that
        $g(u)$ so that $g(u)\frac{dx}{du}du$ is easier to integrate than $f(x)dx$. In general choose $u$ to
        absorb the difficult parts of $f(x)$.
        <br/>
        (Note: All this works because $\frac{d}{dx}$ and $\int{}dx$ are both linear transformations)
